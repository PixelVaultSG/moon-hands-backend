# Negotiation Playbook for Pixel Vault
## Advanced Tactics for Pricing Conversations

---

## THE 7 RULES OF NEGOTIATION

### Rule 1: Never Make the First Concession
**Wrong:** "I can probably do $400"  
**Right:** "Our pricing is already at the market floor. What did you have in mind?"

### Rule 2: Always Trade, Never Give
**Wrong:** "Okay, I'll lower the price"  
**Right:** "I can explore that if we can agree on a 6-month commitment"

### Rule 3: Silence is Power
After stating your position, shut up. Whoever speaks first loses.

### Rule 4: Know Your Walk-Away Price
For Starter: S$347 (floor), $350 (acceptable), $397 (target)  
For Professional: $447 (floor), $467 (acceptable), S$547 (target)

### Rule 5: Never Split the Difference Blindly
**Wrong:** "You want $300, I want S$547, let's meet at $400"  
**Right:** "Here's what I can do at $400... [add conditions]"

### Rule 6: Use Odd Numbers
**Wrong:** "$400"  
**Right:** "$397" - sounds calculated, not made up

### Rule 7: Make Them Work for It
Easy concessions aren't valued. Make them earn every dollar.

## THE NEGOTIATION FRAMEWORK

### Phase 1: Discovery (Don't Negotiate Yet)
Before any numbers, understand:
- What's their current solution costing?
- What's their budget range?
- Who else are they talking to?
- What's their timeline?
- What happens if they don't decide?

**Key Question:** "Before we talk numbers, help me understand what you're comparing us to."

### Phase 2: Anchor (Set the Frame)
**You:** "Our Professional plan is S$547 per month. That includes 2,000 voice minutes, 5,000 WhatsApp messages, and unlimited support."

**Then stop talking.**

### Phase 3: Handle Their Counter
**They say:** "Can you do better?"  
**You say:** "I appreciate you asking. Our pricing is designed to be fair from day one. What were you hoping for?"

**Let them name a number first.**

### Phase 4: The Pivot
**They say:** "$300"  
**You say:** "$300? I can't do that - I'd be losing money. But let me explore what I CAN do..."

### Phase 5: Present Options
Never give one option. Give three.

> "Here's what I can offer:
> 
> **Option A:** $447/month with 6-month commitment (save $300)
> **Option B:** S$547/month with month 2 free if you refer a clinic
> **Option C:** $4,970 annual (get 2 months free)
> 
> Which works best for your situation?"

### Phase 6: The Close
**They say:** "Let me think about it"  
**You say:** "Of course. Just so you know, the 6-month rate expires Friday. After that, it's month-to-month at S$547. Want me to hold that spot?"

## SPECIFIC NEGOTIATION SCENARIOS

### Scenario A: "What's Your Best Price?"

**The Trap:** They want you to bid against yourself.

**Your Response:**
> "Our best price is the one that makes sense for both of us. The published rate is S$547. But I'm willing to explore creative options if there's a commitment on your side. What were you thinking?"

**If they push:**
> "I don't play games with pricing. The rate is S$547. Now, if you're willing to commit to 6 months, I can do $447. If you want to pay annually, it's $4,970. Those are my options. Which works for you?"

---

### Scenario B: "[Competitor] is Cheaper"

**Your Response:**
> "They absolutely are. And you know what? If price is your only criteria, you should go with them. Seriously.
>
> But let me ask - why are you still talking to me? There must be something you're not getting from them."

**Let them explain, then:**
> "Exactly. And that's why we're S$547. We don't cut corners on AI training, support, or reliability. You get what you pay for."

**The close:**
> "Here's what I'll do. Try us for 14 days. If we're not worth the extra $200, go back to them. Fair?"

---

### Scenario C: "We Need a Discount"

**Your Response:**
> "I understand budgets are tight. Instead of discounting, what if I added value?
>
> For the S$547 rate, I'll include:
> - Custom voice training (normally $200)
> - Monthly optimization calls (normally $150/month)
> - Priority WhatsApp support (priceless when you're in a jam)
>
> That's $500+ in extras. Does that help?"

**If they still push:**
> "What if we started with Starter at S$347? You get 500 minutes and 1,000 messages. If you outgrow it, we upgrade. If you don't, you save $200/month. Sound fair?"

---

### Scenario D: "We're a Non-Profit/Startup"

**Your Response:**
> "I respect that. We actually have a startup program. Here's what I can do:
>
> **Months 1-3:** S$347 (Starter rate)
> **Months 4-6:** $397 (bridge rate)
> **Month 7+:** S$547 (standard)
>
> That gives you 6 months to prove ROI before full price. Does that work?"

**Key:** Don't give permanent discounts. Give ramp-up periods.

---

### Scenario E: "We Need This for Multiple Locations"

**Volume Pricing Tiers:**

| Locations | Price/Location | Total | Savings |
|-----------|----------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | S$547 | S$547 | - |
| 2-3 | $447 | $894-1,341 | 10% |
| 4-5 | $397 | $1,588-1,985 | 20% |
| 6-10 | S$347 | $2,082-3,470 | 30% |
| 10+ | Custom | Custom | Contact |

**Your Response:**
> "For multiple locations, we have volume pricing. At 5 locations, you're looking at $397 per location instead of S$547. That's $500/month savings.
>
> Plus, you get:
> - Centralized dashboard for all locations
> - Cross-location analytics
> - Dedicated account manager
> - Priority support
>
> Want me to put together a custom proposal?"

## ADVANCED TACTICS

### The Nibble
After agreeing on price, they ask for "one more thing."

**They say:** "Okay, $447 works. Can you also throw in custom integrations?"

**You say:** "Custom integrations are $500 setup. But since we're doing the 6-month deal, I'll include the first integration free. Additional ones are $500 each. Fair?"

**Key:** Give a little, but charge for extras.

---

### The Krunch
They keep pushing after you've made concessions.

**They say:** "$447 is still high. How about $400?"

**You say:** "Robert, I've already moved from S$547 to $447. That's $300 over 6 months. I'm not going lower, but I AM willing to add value. What would be most helpful - priority support, extra training, or something else?"

**Key:** Stop moving on price. Move on value.

---

### The Bogey
They claim something is a dealbreaker that isn't.

**They say:** "If you can't do $400, we can't move forward."

**You say:** "I understand. And I respect that you have a budget. $400 just doesn't work for us - we'd be losing money. 
>
> Here's what I'll do. I'll hold the $447 rate through Friday. If you find someone who can deliver what we do at $400, take it. If not, you know where to find me. No hard feelings either way."

**Key:** Call their bluff. Be willing to walk.

---

### The Cherry Picker
They want only the features they like at a lower price.

**They say:** "We only need voice, not WhatsApp. Can we get a lower price?"

**You say:** "I hear you. But our pricing is bundled because that's how the economics work. Voice-only would still be $447 because the AI training and infrastructure costs are the same.
>
> Here's the thing - you'll probably use WhatsApp more than you think. Most of our clients get 60% of their inquiries through WhatsApp. But if you truly don't use it, you're not penalized. You still get unlimited voice.
>
> Want to start with voice and add WhatsApp later if needed?"

**Key:** Don't unbundle. Explain the value of the bundle.

## THE SALES REP'S BILL OF RIGHTS

You have the right to:

1. **Say no** to unreasonable demands
2. **Walk away** from bad deals
3. **Ask for commitment** before making concessions
4. **Take time** to consider offers
5. **Set boundaries** on what you'll negotiate
6. **Expect respect** from prospects
7. **Make profit** on every deal

**Remember:** A bad deal is worse than no deal.

## NEGOTIATION MISTAKES TO AVOID

### ❌ Discounting Without Getting Anything
**Wrong:** "Okay, I'll do $400"  
**Right:** "I can do $400 if we agree on 6 months"

### ❌ Apologizing for Your Price
**Wrong:** "I know it's expensive, but..."  
**Right:** "The investment is S$547, and here's what you get..."

### ❌ Justifying with Cost
**Wrong:** "We have to charge this because our costs are high"  
**Right:** "The value is S$547 because of the ROI our clients see"

### ❌ Negotiating Against Yourself
**Wrong:** "I can do S$547... or maybe $447... or how about $400?"  
**Right:** "The rate is S$547. What questions do you have?"

### ❌ Accepting "I'll Think About It"
**Wrong:** "Okay, call me when you're ready"  
**Right:** "What specifically do you need to think about?"

### ❌ Giving Unlimited Revisions
**Wrong:** "Let me know what works and I'll adjust"  
**Right:** "I can offer these three options. Which works best?"

### ❌ Being Desperate
**Wrong:** "Please, I really need this sale"  
**Right:** "I believe this is a good fit, but only if it works for both of us"

## THE NEGOTIATOR'S MINDSET

### Before Every Negotiation:
- [ ] I know my walk-away price
- [ ] I've identified what I can trade
- [ ] I'm willing to say no
- [ ] I'm prepared to walk away
- [ ] I believe in my product's value

### During Negotiation:
- [ ] I'm listening more than talking
- [ ] I'm not getting emotional
- [ ] I'm asking questions, not making statements
- [ ] I'm comfortable with silence
- [ ] I'm trading, not giving

### After Negotiation:
- [ ] I got something in return for every concession
- [ ] The deal is profitable
- [ ] The client feels like they won something
- [ ] I can deliver on what I promised
- [ ] I'm proud of how I handled it

## THE ULTIMATE CLOSE

When all else fails, use this:

> "Look, I'm going to be straight with you. Our price is S$547. I believe it's fair. I believe you'll get 10x value. And I'm willing to prove it with a 14-day trial.
>
> If S$547 is truly a dealbreaker, I respect that. We're not the cheapest, and we don't want to be. But if you're looking for the best value, that's us.
>
> What do you say we start the trial and let the results speak for themselves?"

**Then shut up. Wait for them to respond.**
