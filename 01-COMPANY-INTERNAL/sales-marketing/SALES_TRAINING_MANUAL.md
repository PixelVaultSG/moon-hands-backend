# MODULE 1: SCENARIO-BASED SALES SCRIPTS

## SCENARIO 1: Client Already Has an Agent

### The Situation
Prospect says: *"We already have a receptionist/answering service/AI solution."*

### The Mindset
Don't compete on features. Compete on **outcomes** and **hidden costs**.

### The Script

**Opening (Acknowledge + Pivot):**
> "That's great that you're already investing in customer communication - it shows you understand how important every inquiry is. Can I ask, how's that working out for you? Are you capturing every after-hours call and WhatsApp message?"

**Discovery Questions:**
1. "What happens when your receptionist is on lunch, sick, or after 6 PM?"
2. "How quickly do you respond to WhatsApp inquiries right now?"
3. "What's your current cost per month for reception coverage?"
4. "Do you know how many calls you miss each week?"

**The Pivot (Uncover Pain):**
> "Most clinics I speak with tell me the same thing - their current solution handles the basics, but they're still missing 30-40% of inquiries. Here's what I mean...
> 
> **After-hours calls** - When your receptionist goes home, those calls either go to voicemail or an answering service that just takes messages. Our AI actually books appointments 24/7.
>
> **WhatsApp response time** - The average person expects a reply within 5 minutes on WhatsApp. If it's longer, they're already calling your competitor. Our AI responds instantly.
>
> **Cost comparison** - A full-time receptionist costs $2,500-3,500/month plus CPF. Even an answering service is $500-800. Our Professional plan is S$547 and handles unlimited volume."

**The Trial Close:**
> "I'm not asking you to fire anyone or cancel anything today. What if we ran a 14-day trial alongside your current setup? You can compare the results side-by-side. Fair enough?"

### Objection Handlers

**"We just signed a contract with [competitor]"**
> "I understand. Most clinics don't realize that many AI solutions lock you into long contracts with hidden usage fees. Our pricing is transparent - S$547 flat, no surprises. Would it hurt to see what else is out there before you're locked in for a year?"

**"Our receptionist is family/loyal employee"**
> "That's actually wonderful - loyalty is rare. We're not replacing your receptionist, we're giving them superpowers. Imagine if they never had to answer repetitive questions about pricing or hours. They could focus on patients who are actually in the clinic. Wouldn't that be better for everyone?"

## SCENARIO 2: Price Objection & Rejection

### The Situation
Prospect says: *"That's too expensive"* or *"We don't have the budget"*

### The Mindset
Price is never the real objection. It's about **perceived value** and **risk**.

### The Script

**First Response (Don't Defend, Discover):**
> "I hear you. Can I ask - when you say expensive, are you comparing this to what you're currently spending on reception, or is it more about not being sure of the ROI?"

**If They Say "No Budget":**
> "I understand budgets are tight. Let me ask differently - how much is a new patient worth to your clinic over their lifetime?"
> *[Let them answer - typically $2,000-5,000 for dental]*
> "So if our AI books just ONE extra patient per month, it pays for itself 4-10x over. Last month, our average dental client booked 12 new patients through the AI. That's a 2,400% return."

**The Reframe:**
> "Let me reframe this. You're not spending S$547 - you're investing S$547 to capture leads that are currently going to your competitors. Every missed call is potentially $2,000 walking out the door."

### Counteroffer Strategies

**Option 1: The Starter Trial**
> "What if we started with our Starter plan at S$347? That's less than $10 a day - less than a coffee shop lunch. You get 500 voice minutes and 1,000 WhatsApp messages. If you see results, we can upgrade. If not, you cancel. Zero risk."

**Option 2: Performance Guarantee (Use Carefully)**
> "Tell you what - I'm confident this will work for you. If we don't book at least 5 appointments in your first 30 days, I'll refund your first month. Deal?"

**Option 3: The Payment Plan**
> "Would it help if we split the payment? $250 now, $250 in 30 days after you've seen it working?"

**Option 4: The Value Stack**
> "Let me show you what S$547 actually gets you:
> - 24/7 AI receptionist (worth $3,000/month if human)
> - Instant WhatsApp responses (worth $500/month in staff time)
> - Lead qualification (worth $1,000/month in sales efficiency)
> - Real-time analytics (worth $200/month)
> 
> That's $4,700 in value for S$547. And that's not marketing fluff - those are actual costs you'd pay otherwise."

### The "Let Me Think About It" Handler

> "Of course, take your time. But can I ask - what specifically are you thinking about? Is it the price, or are you unsure if this will work for your specific clinic?"
> *[Let them answer]*
> "Here's what I've found - the clinics that 'think about it' usually fall into two camps. Either they're genuinely evaluating options (which is smart), or they're worried about making the wrong decision. Which camp are you in?"
> 
> **If evaluating:** "What other options are you considering? I'd be happy to give you an honest comparison."
> 
> **If worried:** "I get it. What if we removed the risk entirely? Start with a 7-day free trial. No credit card, no commitment. If you don't love it, we part as friends. Sound fair?"

## SCENARIO 3: Open Pricing Negotiation

### The Situation
Prospect says: *"Can you do better on price?"* or *"What's your best price?"*

### The Mindset
Never discount without getting something in return. Create a trade, not a giveaway.

### The Script

**The Trade Framework:**
> "I appreciate you asking. Here's the thing - our pricing is already at the market floor. We make our margin on volume, not markup. But I'm willing to explore options. What did you have in mind?"
> *[Let them make the first offer]*

**If They Lowball (e.g., "Can you do $200?"):**
> "$200? I can't do that - I'd be losing money on every call. But here's what I CAN do...
>
> **Option A:** If you commit to 6 months upfront, I can do $447/month - that's $300 saved over the term.
>
> **Option B:** If you refer one other clinic that signs up, I'll give you your second month free.
>
> **Option C:** If you pay annually, it's $4,970 instead of $5,964 - that's basically 2 months free.
>
> Which of those works better for you?"

**The "Meet in the Middle" Strategy:**
> "You want $200, I need S$547. What if we meet at $397 for the first 3 months? That's $100 off per month to prove the value. After 3 months, if you're happy, we go to standard pricing. Fair?"

**The "Add Value Instead of Discount" Strategy:**
> "I can't lower the price, but I CAN add value. What if I include:
> - Custom voice training for your clinic (normally $200)
> - Priority support with direct WhatsApp to me
> - Monthly strategy calls to optimize your scripts
> 
> That's $500+ in extras at no charge. Does that work?"

### Negotiation Anchors

**Never say these:**
- ❌ "Our normal price is..." (implies you're inflating)
- ❌ "I need to check with my manager" (weakens position)
- ❌ "That's our best price" (invites them to push harder)

**Always say these:**
- ✅ "Our pricing is designed to be fair from day one"
- ✅ "I can explore creative options, but I can't go below cost"
- ✅ "What would make this a no-brainer for you?"

## SCENARIO 4: The Fence-Sitter (50-50 Client)

### The Situation
Prospect seems interested but won't commit. Says things like:
- *"Sounds good, let me get back to you"*
- *"I need to discuss with my partner"*
- *"Maybe next quarter"*

### The Mindset
Fence-sitters fear making the wrong decision more than they desire the solution. **Reduce the decision size.**

### The Script

**The Micro-Commitment Approach:**
> "I can tell this makes sense to you, but something's holding you back. Can I be direct - is it the commitment, or are you not convinced it will work?"
> *[Let them answer]*

**If It's Commitment Fear:**
> "What if we made this ridiculously easy? No contract, cancel anytime, 14-day free trial. You literally have nothing to lose except missed calls. What's the worst that happens - you try it, don't like it, cancel? But what if it books you 10 new patients next month?"

**If It's Partner/Spouse Approval:**
> "Totally understand. What questions do you think they'll have? Let me give you the answers so you're prepared."
> *[Address each concern]*
> "Here's what I suggest - let's set up the trial now. In 14 days, you'll have real data to show them. Numbers don't lie. Sound good?"

**If It's "Next Quarter":**
> "I get timing is important. But can I ask - how many calls and WhatsApp messages will you miss between now and next quarter?"
> *[Calculate: ~90 days × 5 missed inquiries/day = 450 missed opportunities]*
> "That's potentially 450 people calling your competitors. Even if you only convert 10% of those, that's 45 patients. At $2,000 lifetime value, that's $90,000 walking out the door. For a S$547 investment."

### The Urgency Creator (Use Ethically)

**Scarcity:**
> "I'm only taking on 10 dental clinics in Singapore this quarter to ensure quality. I have 3 spots left. Not to pressure you, but if you're genuinely interested, I'd recommend securing your spot."

**Loss Aversion:**
> "Every day you wait is a day your competitors are capturing those after-hours inquiries. The clinics that started with us in January have already booked 200+ appointments through the AI. That's 200 patients that could have been yours."

### The Assumed Close for Fence-Sitters

> "Based on what you've told me, I genuinely believe this will work for Bright Smile Dental. Here's what I propose - let's start your trial today. I'll personally walk you through setup, and we'll have you live by tomorrow. What's a good time for our onboarding call - morning or afternoon?"
> *[Assume the yes, ask for logistics]*

# MODULE 2: OBJECTION LIBRARY

## Common Objections & Responses

### "I Need to Think About It"
> "Absolutely. Quick question - what specifically do you need to think about? The price, the technology, or whether it will work for your clinic?"
> *[Isolate the objection, then address it]*

### "Send Me More Information"
> "Happy to. What specific information would be most helpful? I want to make sure I'm sending you exactly what you need, not a generic brochure."
> *[Often reveals real concern]*

### "We're Too Small for This"
> "Actually, smaller clinics benefit MORE. You don't have a team to cover phones 24/7. Every missed call hurts more. One new patient per month covers the cost."

### "We're Too Big/Complex for This"
> "That's exactly why you need this. At your volume, you're probably missing 20+ inquiries daily. Let me show you how we handle multi-location setups..."

### "AI Sounds Impersonal"
> "I used to think that too. But our AI is trained on thousands of real conversations. Most patients can't tell the difference - and they love the instant response. Want to hear a sample call?"

### "What If It Doesn't Work?"
> "Fair concern. That's why we have the 14-day trial. You'll know within a week if this is working. And if it's not, you cancel, no hard feelings. But here's the thing - in 6 months of operation, we've never had a dental clinic cancel because it 'didn't work.' They cancel because they outgrew us and built their own system."

### "I Had a Bad Experience with [Similar Product]"
> "I'm sorry to hear that. What went wrong?"
> *[Listen carefully]*
> "That's frustrating. Here's how we're different... [address specific concern]. Would it help to talk to another clinic owner who switched from them to us?"

# MODULE 3: ADVANCED TACTICS

## The "Reverse Close"
Use when prospect is being difficult or non-committal.

> "You know what, I'm getting the feeling this might not be the right fit. And that's totally okay - we're not for everyone. Can I ask - is there something I'm missing, or is this just not a priority right now?"
> *[Creates psychological space, often makes them defend why they ARE interested]*

## The "Takeaway"
Use when prospect is being demanding or disrespectful.

> "I want to be honest with you - I'm not sure we can help you. Our best clients see this as a partnership, not just a transaction. If you're looking for the cheapest option, we're probably not it. But if you want a system that genuinely books more appointments, I'm your guy. Which camp are you in?"

## The "Puppy Dog Close"
Let them try it with no commitment.

> "Here's what I'll do - I'll set you up with a fully functional demo using your actual business info. No credit card, no contract. Use it for a week. If you don't book at least 3 appointments, delete my number. If you do, we talk about making it permanent. Deal?"

## The "Ben Franklin Close"
Help them weigh pros and cons visually.

> "Let's do this together. Grab a piece of paper. On one side, write down what happens if you do this. On the other, what happens if you don't. I'll start...
> 
> **If you do this:** Capture after-hours calls, instant WhatsApp responses, never miss an inquiry, free up staff time, look more professional...
> 
> **If you don't:** Keep missing calls, slow WhatsApp responses, lose patients to competitors, staff stays overwhelmed...
> 
> Which list is longer?"

# MODULE 5: CLOSING FRAMEWORKS

## The 3-Option Close
Never give a yes/no choice. Always give 3 options.

> "Based on what you've told me, here are your options:
> 
> **Option 1:** Starter Plan at S$347 - good for testing the waters
> **Option 2:** Professional Plan at S$547 - best value, handles your full volume
> **Option 3:** Do nothing - keep handling calls manually, miss after-hours inquiries
> 
> Which feels right for where you are?"

## The Summary Close
Recap everything they've agreed to.

> "Let me make sure I have this right. You said:
> - You're missing calls after 6 PM
> - WhatsApp inquiries take hours to respond to
> - Your receptionist is overwhelmed
> - You want to book more consultations
> 
> Our AI solves all of that for S$547/month. Should we get started?"

## The Question Close
Turn the close into a question about implementation.

> "Assuming we move forward, would you want to start with voice calls, WhatsApp, or both at once?"
> *[Assumes the sale, asks for logistics]*

# MODULE 6: POST-CALL FOLLOW-UP

## If They Said "Yes"
1. Send immediate confirmation with next steps
2. Schedule onboarding call within 24 hours
3. Send calendar invite
4. Add to CRM with notes
5. Send welcome email with prep checklist

## If They Said "No"
1. Thank them for their time
2. Ask permission to follow up in 30 days
3. Add to nurture sequence
4. Connect on LinkedIn
5. Send occasional value (case studies, tips)

## If They Said "Maybe"
1. Send summary email within 1 hour
2. Include trial signup link
3. Add to follow-up sequence (3, 7, 14 days)
4. Offer to answer any questions
5. Send relevant case study

# MODULE 7: RED FLAGS & WHEN TO WALK AWAY

## Don't Work With These Clients:
1. **Price-focused only** - Will churn at first cheaper option
2. **Disrespectful** - Life's too short
3. **Unrealistic expectations** - "Book me 100 patients in a week"
4. **No decision-making power** - Can't get budget approved
5. **Bad fit** - Your solution genuinely won't help them

## The Graceful Exit:
> "You know what, after our conversation, I'm not sure we're the best fit for what you need. I'd rather be honest than sell you something that won't work. But if things change, feel free to reach out."

# TRAINING EXERCISES

## Exercise 1: Role Play
Practice each scenario with a partner. Switch roles (sales rep vs. prospect).

## Exercise 2: Objection Judo
Write down 10 objections. For each, write 3 different responses.

## Exercise 3: The Silent Close
After your pitch, stay silent for 10 seconds. Count in your head. Whoever speaks first loses.

## Exercise 4: Record Yourself
Record 5 practice calls. Listen back. Note:
- How many times did you say "um" or "uh"?
- Did you talk more than 40% of the time?
- Did you ask good discovery questions?
- Did you rush to pitch or truly understand their pain?
