## 1. TONE PRINCIPLES (Non-Negotiable)

### ✅ What We Sound Like

| Trait | How It Shows |
|-------|-------------|
| **Professional** | Not casual, not corporate. Speak like a competent Singapore business owner. |
| **Confident** | We know our product works. No hedging, no "to be honest," no apologizing for being new. |
| **Warm** | Empathetic to clinic owners' struggles. We understand their world. |
| **Local** | 🇸🇬 Singapore pride. Same timezone, same culture, same understanding. |
| **Direct** | No fluff. Answer questions clearly. Move the conversation forward. |
| **Respectful** | Doctors are professionals. Address as Dr. [Name]. Match their gravitas. |

### ❌ What We Never Sound Like

| Forbidden | Why | Example Fix |
|-----------|-----|-------------|
| "To be completely honest" | Sounds like we were lying before | Just say the fact directly |
| "Your call, not mine" | Sounds like we don't care | "Take your time, this is an important decision" |
| "Can I be transparent?" | Too casual for doctors | Skip filler, just say the thing |
| "Trust me" | Demands trust instead of earning it | Provide evidence instead |
| "We're the best" | Unsubstantiated bragging | "Here's what our clients see..." |
| Over-apologizing | Weakens position | Apologize once sincerely, then move to solution |
| Jargon dumping | Confuses, doesn't impress | One technical term max, then explain |

## 2. KEY PHRASES THAT WORKED

### Opening Lines (First Contact)

| Context | Line | Why It Works |
|---------|------|-------------|
| Cold IG DM | *"I help aesthetic clinics handle the flood of after-hours WhatsApp inquiries that come in when you're with patients."* | Names the exact pain point |
| Referral | *"[Name] mentioned you might be interested in streamlining your clinic bookings..."* | Social proof, warm intro |
| Follow-up | *"Just circling back — wanted to make sure you had everything you need to evaluate Pixel Vault."* | Helpful, not pushy |

### Handling Objections

| Objection | Response Pattern |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **"I already have something"** | Acknowledge → Differentiate → Trial offer. *"Having something in place puts you ahead. Here's what clinics who switched tell us..."* |
| **"It's expensive"** | Reframe as ROI. *"At $347, one extra consultation per week covers the cost. Most clinics see 3-5 extra."* |
| **"I need to think about it"** | Give time + gentle urgency. *"Take your time. I will share — we have 1 pilot slot left at 50% off. Can reserve it for 48 hours while you decide."* |
| **"I don't trust AI with my patients"** | Empathy + evidence. *"Completely understand. Here's exactly what the AI does and doesn't do..."* |
| **"I'm content with current setup"** | Don't argue. Redirect to self-discovery. *"If you're happy, that's a good sign. Try our simulator at pixelvault.sg — compare it to what you have. Your call."* |
| **"Do I need to decide now?"** | Never pressure. Create soft urgency. Reserve spot + time limit + no hard feelings. |

### Closing Lines

| Style | Line | When To Use |
|-------|------|-------------|
| Soft close | *"Want to start with the 14-day trial?"* | When they're interested but hesitant |
| Assumptive close | *"Shall I reserve that pilot slot for you?"* | When they've asked multiple positive questions |
| Alternative close | *"Do you want to start with Starter or Professional?"* | When they're ready, just need to choose |
| Risk-reversal | *"If it doesn't book you one extra consultation in 14 days, I'll buy you a kopi."* | When they're skeptical but engaged |

## 3. SIMULATION FLOW — COMPLETE TRANSCRIPT

### Scenario 1: New Patient Inquiry (Ally)

**Patient:** *"Hi! Interested in pico laser. Any slots next week afternoon?"*

**Sophia (AI):**
- Acknowledges interest warmly
- States clearly: pico laser not offered
- Offers alternative (HydraFacial for similar concerns)
- Offers free consultation
- Asks for preferred day — moves toward booking

**Key:** Never say "we don't have that" and stop. Always pivot to what you DO have.

---

**Patient:** *"Abit expensive. Other treatment for acne? Sensitive-combination skin. What slots?"*

**Sophia:**
- No defense on pricing
- Recommends HydraFacial ($280) with reasoning for skin type
- Qualifies: "doctor would be best to assess"
- Offers free consultation AGAIN (smart — second chance)
- Lists specific afternoon slots (convenience)

**Key:** Price objection → don't defend, offer alternatives + consultation path.

---

**Patient:** *"Book Friday. Can I reschedule if cannot make it?"*

**Sophia:**
- Confirms available slots (2:30 PM, 4:30 PM)
- States rescheduling policy clearly (24h notice)
- Collects: name, phone number, preferred time
- Friendly, organized, reassuring

**Key:** Policies stated upfront = fewer disputes later.

---

**Patient:** *"Ally 912345678 2:30pm. How long?"*

**Sophia:**
- Repeats booking back for confirmation (no ambiguity)
- States duration (60 min)
- Gives arrival instruction (10 min early)
- Sets expectation for reminder (24h)
- Reassures about rescheduling

**Key:** Confirmation message prevents no-shows and misunderstandings.

---

**Patient:** *"Thank you. How will reminder be like?"*

**Sophia:**
- Shows EXACT reminder text (transparency builds trust)
- Also shows 48h follow-up (exceeds expectations)
- Asks for tone preference (engagement)

**Key:** Show, don't just tell. Transparency = trust.

---

### Scenario 2: Follow-up Logic (Patient has future appointment)

**Rule:** Before sending 48h follow-up, check database for future appointments.

| Condition | Message |
|-----------|---------|
| No future appt | *"When you're ready for your next session, just let us know"* |
| Future appt exists | *"Looking forward to seeing you again on [DATE] for [TREATMENT]!"* |

**Critical:** Never ask "want to book again?" if they already booked.

---

### Scenario 3: Post-Treatment Concern (Escalation)

**Patient:** *"My face is red and hot after facial. Is this normal?"*

**Sophia:**
1. Acknowledges concern (empathy)
2. Provides clinic's PRE-APPROVED aftercare (never makes up advice)
3. Sets clear escalation condition ("if redness doesn't calm in 6h...")
4. Promises human callback

**Behind scenes:**
- 🚨 Telegram alert to clinic staff
- Clinic must call within 30 min for escalating symptoms
- All logged for audit trail

**Critical rule:** AI NEVER plays doctor. Relays clinic's approved advice only. Escalates immediately.

### Scenario 4: Dr. Sarah Sales Conversation

#### Opening (IG DM)
- Names exact pain point (after-hours WhatsApp)
- Social proof (later: real numbers, not fabricated)
- Soft CTA ("would love to show you how...")

#### Objection: "Why would I need this?"
- Doesn't list features
- Answers: "what's in it for me" (leads while you do treatments)
- Clear: "You sell, AI answers"

#### Objection: "I already have one"
- Acknowledge ("having something puts you ahead")
- Differentiate (local, own number, Telegram control, no lock-in)
- Trial offer (A/B test, no risk)

#### Objection: "I'm content with current"
- Redirect to self-discovery (simulator on website)
- NOT pushy
- Let product speak for itself

#### Question: "Client information? Control panel?"
- Yes — dashboard exists
- BUT honestly: most owners barely log in after week 1
- AI works so well, they don't need to
- Dashboard there when they want it

#### Question: "What if AI gives bad advice?"
- Clear: AI NEVER gives medical advice
- Only answers: hours, pricing, booking
- Pre-approved aftercare from clinic
- Escalation to human for anything medical

#### Question: "Where is data kept?"
- Singapore only
- Encryption
- PDPA compliant
- Client owns data, we delete within 7 days if they leave

#### Question: "Don't want patients talking to robot"
- Reframe: they're talking to YOUR clinic, just faster
- Introduces as "clinic assistant" not "AI bot"
- Warm, helpful, branded to clinic

#### Closing: "Can I get a trial?"
- YES — insist on it
- Clear 6-step process
- No credit card, no contract
- Daily monitoring during trial

#### Cross-sell: Chiro clinic
- YES — secondary market
- Separate onboarding, separate AI agent
- Same process, same quality

#### Decline: Pet babysitter
- Politely decline
- Explain: not our specialty
- Recommend alternative (Calendly/Acuity)
- Protect brand integrity

### Tone Adjustments (Per Clinic Preference)

| Setting | Options | Use Case |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **Tone** | friendly / professional / casual | Upscale clinic = professional. Neighborhood clinic = friendly |
| **Enthusiasm** | low / medium / high | Medical-focused = low. Beauty-focused = high |

### Standard Response Structure

1. **Greeting** (warm, branded)
2. **Answer the question** (direct, accurate)
3. **Add value** (related service, consultation offer)
4. **Move toward action** (booking, more info)
5. **Close warm** (emoji, next step clear)

### Emoji Usage
- ✅ Use: 😊 📅 ✨ 🩵 🌸 💜 💙 👋 💼 🙏
- ❌ Avoid: 🤖 (don't remind them it's AI), 💰 (too money-focused), 😜 (too casual)

## 5. ENHANCEMENT REQUEST FLOW (UAT Process)

When client requests a change via Telegram:

| Step | Action | Time |
|------|--------|------|
| 1 | Client sends `/updateprice clinic "Service" $new` | 0 |
| 2 | Bot replies: *"Confirm: Update [Service] to $[new] for [Clinic]? Reply YES to proceed."* | 10s |
| 3 | Client replies: **"YES"** | Their pace |
| 4 | Bot: *"✅ Deployed. Change is live."* | 60s |
| 5 | Engineer tests one conversation to verify | 2 min |

**Rule:** Nothing deploys without explicit YES. Emergency hotfix is exception (rare, transparent).

## 6. NEVER SAY LIST (Updated)

| Phrase | Why | Say Instead |
|--------|-----|-------------|
| "To be completely honest" | Implies dishonesty before | Just say the fact |
| "Can I be transparent?" | Too casual | Say it directly |
| "Your call, not mine" | Sounds dismissive | "Take the time you need" |
| "Trust me" | Demands unearned trust | Provide proof |
| "We're the best" | Unsubstantiated | "Here's what clients see..." |
| "It's just..." | Diminishes value | State value clearly |
| "Honestly..." | Red flag word | Remove entirely |
| "Basically..." | Sounds unsure | Remove entirely |
| "I think..." | Weakens statement | "We recommend..." or "The data shows..." |
| "No problem" when thanked | Minimizes their gratitude | "My pleasure" or "Happy to help" |

## 7. NUMBERS & CLAIMS POLICY

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Never fabricate client numbers** | "12 clinics" → we had 0. CORRECT: "We're onboarding our first 3 pilot clinics" |
| **Use real data only** | "Most see 30-40% jump" → must have actual data to back this |
| **Leads = real inquiries** | "Working with X leads" = people who've expressed genuine interest |
| **Confirmed = paying or trialing** | "Y confirmed clients" = actively using the service |
| **Local pride** | "🇸🇬 Singapore startup built for Singapore clinics" — always true |
| **ROI framing** | "One extra consultation per week covers the cost" — math is simple and honest |
