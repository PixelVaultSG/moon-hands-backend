# Sales Role-Play Scenarios
## Practice Drills for AI Sales Rep Training

---

## SCENARIO 1: The Stubborn "We Have Someone" Client

### Setup
**Prospect:** Dr. Lim, Bright Smile Dental  
**Personality:** Defensive, loyal to current receptionist  
**Opening Line:** "We already have a receptionist who's been with us for 5 years. We don't need AI."

### Your Mission
Get them to admit pain points without attacking their receptionist.

### Expected Flow

**You:** "That's wonderful - loyalty is rare these days. Sarah sounds like a valuable team member. Can I ask, what happens when Sarah is on her lunch break or takes a sick day?"

**Dr. Lim:** "We have an answering service for after hours."

**You:** "Got it. And how quickly do they respond to WhatsApp messages?"

**Dr. Lim:** "Well... we don't really check WhatsApp that often. Maybe once or twice a day."

**You:** "Interesting. And how many calls would you estimate you miss during lunch hours or when Sarah's busy with a patient?"

**Dr. Lim:** "I don't know, maybe a few a day?"

**You:** "So let's say 3-4 missed calls per day. That's 20+ per week, 80+ per month. Even if you only convert 10% of those, that's 8 patients. At $2,000 lifetime value, that's $16,000 walking out the door every month. For context, our AI costs $547."

**Dr. Lim:** "But I don't want to replace Sarah."

**You:** "And you shouldn't! Sarah's great with patients who are IN the clinic. What if AI handled the repetitive stuff - pricing questions, hours, booking - so Sarah could focus on the people right in front of her?"

### Success Criteria
- [ ] Didn't attack the receptionist
- [ ] Uncovered pain (WhatsApp, missed calls)
- [ ] Quantified the cost of inaction
- [ ] Positioned AI as supporting, not replacing
- [ ] Offered trial

## SCENARIO 2: The Price Negotiator

### Setup
**Prospect:** Marcus, Elite Fitness SG  
**Personality:** Hard negotiator, tests boundaries  
**Opening Line:** "$547 is way too much. I can get a virtual assistant from the Philippines for $300/month."

### Your Mission
Don't discount. Show value difference.

### Expected Flow

**You:** "You're absolutely right - you can get cheaper labor. But let me ask, does that VA answer calls at 2 AM? Do they respond to WhatsApp in 30 seconds? Can they handle 10 simultaneous conversations?"

**Marcus:** "Well, no, but..."

**You:** "Here's the thing - our AI isn't competing with humans on price. It's competing on availability and scale. A human works 8 hours, takes breaks, gets sick, needs training. Our AI works 24/7, never calls in sick, and is already trained on thousands of fitness inquiries."

**Marcus:** "Still, $547 is a lot for a small gym."

**You:** "I get it. Let me ask - how much is a new membership worth to you?"

**Marcus:** "About $1,200 a year."

**You:** "So if our AI signs up just ONE extra member per month, it pays for itself 2.4x over. Last month, our average fitness client signed up 8 new members through the AI. That's $9,600 in revenue for a $547 investment."

**Marcus:** "Can you do $300?"

**You:** "I can't go to $300 - I'd be losing money. But here's what I CAN do. If you commit to 6 months upfront, I'll do $447/month. That's $300 saved. Or, if you refer another gym that signs up, I'll give you month 2 free. Which works better?"

### Success Criteria
- [ ] Didn't immediately discount
- [ ] Compared apples to apples (24/7 vs 8 hours)
- [ ] Quantified ROI with their numbers
- [ ] Offered trade, not giveaway
- [ ] Gave options

## SCENARIO 3: The Fence-Sitter

### Setup
**Prospect:** Jennifer, Glow Aesthetics  
**Personality:** Indecisive, needs reassurance  
**Opening Line:** "This sounds interesting... but I need to think about it. Can you send me some information?"

### Your Mission
Get them to articulate what's really holding them back.

### Expected Flow

**You:** "Of course, Jennifer. Before I send anything, can I ask - what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the price, or are you not sure if this will work for aesthetic clinics?"

**Jennifer:** "I don't know... I just need to think about it."

**You:** "I understand. Let me be direct with you - when people say they need to think about it, it usually means one of three things. Either they're evaluating other options, they're worried about making the wrong decision, or it's just not a priority right now. Which one is it for you?"

**Jennifer:** "I guess I'm worried about making the wrong decision. I've been burned by software before."

**You:** "I'm sorry to hear that. What happened?"

**Jennifer:** "We signed up for this scheduling software that promised the world, and it was a nightmare. Cost us thousands."

**You:** "That's frustrating. Here's how we're different - you don't pay anything for 14 days. You get to see exactly how many appointments it books before you commit a single dollar. And if you don't love it, you walk away. No contract, no hard feelings. Does that help with the concern?"

**Jennifer:** "Yeah, that does sound better."

**You:** "Great. What if we just started the trial today? In 14 days, you'll have real data instead of having to guess. Sound fair?"

### Success Criteria
- [ ] Didn't accept "I need to think about it"
- [ ] Got them to articulate the real concern
- [ ] Listened to their past experience
- [ ] Addressed concern with trial offer
- [ ] Asked for micro-commitment (trial)

## SCENARIO 4: The "Too Small" Objection

### Setup
**Prospect:** Dr. Tan, Solo Dental Practice  
**Personality:** Humble, thinks they're not big enough  
**Opening Line:** "I'm just a one-person practice. This seems like it's for bigger clinics."

### Your Mission
Show them why small practices benefit MORE.

### Expected Flow

**You:** "Dr. Tan, can I be honest with you? Small practices are our BEST clients. Here's why - you don't have a team to cover phones. When you're with a patient, every call goes to voicemail. When you're doing admin at night, you can't answer WhatsApp."

**Dr. Tan:** "But I don't get that many calls."

**You:** "How many would you say you miss per week?"

**Dr. Tan:** "Maybe 10-15?"

**You:** "So 50-60 per month. Even at a 20% booking rate, that's 10-12 patients. At $2,000 lifetime value, that's $20,000-24,000 you're leaving on the table. For a $547 investment."

**Dr. Tan:** "But I can't handle that many new patients anyway."

**You:** "That's a good problem to have! What if you could be more selective? Take only the high-value cases - implants, Invisalign, full mouth reconstructions. Let the AI pre-qualify everyone and only book the premium consultations."

**Dr. Tan:** "Hmm, I hadn't thought about it that way."

**You:** "You're in a unique position - you can be selective. Bigger clinics can't. They have to take everything. You can curate your perfect patient mix. Want to see how that would work?"

### Success Criteria
- [ ] Reframed "too small" as advantage
- [ ] Quantified missed opportunity
- [ ] Addressed capacity concern
- [ ] Positioned AI as qualification tool
- [ ] Offered demo

## SCENARIO 5: The Competitor's Client

### Setup
**Prospect:** David, Wellness Center  
**Personality:** Skeptical, been burned before  
**Opening Line:** "We tried something like this before with [Competitor X]. It was terrible. The AI was robotic and patients complained."

### Your Mission
Differentiate without trashing competitor. Let them vent.

### Expected Flow

**You:** "I'm sorry to hear that, David. That sounds frustrating. What specifically went wrong?"

**David:** "The AI sounded like a robot. Patients knew immediately. And it couldn't handle basic questions. We got so many complaints."

**You:** "That makes sense. A lot of early AI solutions used generic voices and scripts. They were basically fancy voicemail. Here's what we do differently...
>
> First, our AI is trained on thousands of real conversations. It uses natural language, not scripts. It can handle interruptions, accents, even slang.
>
> Second, we custom-train it for YOUR business. It knows your services, your pricing, your booking rules. It's not generic.
>
> Third - and this is the big one - you can hear it before you commit. Want me to play you a sample call right now?"

**David:** "Okay, sure."

**[Play sample call]**

**David:** "Wow, that's actually pretty good. Much better than what we had."

**You:** "Right? And here's the thing - if it doesn't sound perfect for YOUR clinic, we'll keep training it until it does. We don't go live until you're 100% happy with how it sounds. Does that address your concern?"

**David:** "Yeah, that does."

**You:** "What if we set up a custom demo using your actual business info? You can hear exactly how it would sound for Wellness Center. No commitment. Sound good?"

### Success Criteria
- [ ] Let them vent fully
- [ ] Asked what specifically went wrong
- [ ] Differentiated without trashing competitor
- [ ] Offered proof (sample call)
- [ ] Offered custom demo

## SCENARIO 6: The "Call Me Next Quarter" Delay

### Setup
**Prospect:** Michelle, Busy Clinic Owner  
**Personality:** Overwhelmed, procrastinator  
**Opening Line:** "This sounds great, but we're swamped right now. Can you call me back in January?"

### Your Mission
Create urgency without being pushy. Quantify the cost of waiting.

### Expected Flow

**You:** "I totally understand, Michelle. You're busy. Can I ask - how many calls and WhatsApp messages do you think you'll miss between now and January?"

**Michelle:** "I don't know, I haven't really thought about it."

**You:** "Let's do some quick math. It's October now, so that's about 90 days until January. If you miss even 5 inquiries per day - which is conservative - that's 450 missed opportunities.
>
> Even if you only convert 10% of those, that's 45 patients. At $1,500 average value, that's $67,500 in revenue. For a $547/month investment.
>
> I'm not saying this to pressure you. I'm saying it because I genuinely think you'd benefit from starting now rather than waiting."

**Michelle:** "But I really don't have time to set this up right now."

**You:** "What if I told you setup takes 30 minutes? I do all the heavy lifting. You just answer some questions about your business, and I handle the rest. We can have you live by tomorrow."

**Michelle:** "Really? That fast?"

**You:** "Really. And here's what I'll do - if you start now, I'll personally monitor your first week and optimize everything. You'll have my direct WhatsApp. Deal?"

### Success Criteria
- [ ] Quantified cost of waiting
- [ ] Addressed time concern
- [ ] Made setup sound easy
- [ ] Offered personal support
- [ ] Asked for commitment

## SCENARIO 7: The "Need to Ask My Partner" Stall

### Setup
**Prospect:** James, Co-Owner of Medical Practice  
**Personality:** Non-confrontational, defers to partner  
**Opening Line:** "I like this, but I need to run it by my business partner. Can I get back to you?"

### Your Mission
Arm them with everything they need to sell their partner.

### Expected Flow

**You:** "Absolutely, James. What questions do you think your partner will have?"

**James:** "Probably the cost, and whether it actually works."

**You:** "Fair. Let me give you everything you need to answer those."
>
> **For the cost question:** "The Professional plan is $547/month. Our current reception coverage costs $2,200/month plus CPF. This replaces that at 22% of the cost."
>
> **For the 'does it work' question:** "We offer a 14-day trial. Zero risk. If it doesn't book at least 5 appointments, we pay nothing. Plus, here are 3 case studies from similar practices."
>
> **The kicker:** "And if we sign up this week, we get the custom voice training included - that's normally $200."

**James:** "Okay, that helps."

**You:** "Here's what I suggest. Let's start the trial NOW. In 14 days, you'll have real data to show your partner - not promises, actual appointments booked. Numbers don't lie. If your partner hates it, cancel. Sound fair?"

**James:** "Actually, that's a good idea. Let's do that."

### Success Criteria
- [ ] Asked what partner will ask
- [ ] Armed them with answers
- [ ] Provided social proof
- [ ] Added urgency (this week bonus)
- [ ] Offered trial as proof

## SCENARIO 8: The Aggressive Negotiator

### Setup
**Prospect:** Robert, Chain Clinic Owner  
**Personality:** Bully, tries to intimidate  
**Opening Line:** "Here's the thing. I need this for 5 locations. I'll give you $200 per location, take it or leave it. That's $1,000/month."

### Your Mission
Stand firm. Don't let them bully you. Either walk away or trade.

### Expected Flow

**You:** "Robert, I appreciate the directness. But I can't do $200 per location - I'd be losing money on every call."

**Robert:** "Then we're done."

**You:** "Hang on. Let me explore options. For 5 locations, here's what I CAN do...
>
> **Option 1:** $400 per location ($2,000 total) with annual prepay. You get 2 months free. Effective rate: $333/location.
>
> **Option 2:** $450 per location ($2,250 total) with 6-month commitment. Plus I'll include white-labeling so it looks like your own system.
>
> **Option 3:** We stick at $547, but I add dedicated account management, monthly strategy calls, and priority support.
>
> Which of those works for you?"

**Robert:** "$400 is still too high."

**You:** "I understand. Here's the thing - I'm not trying to squeeze you. Our pricing is what it is because we invest heavily in AI training, support, and infrastructure. If you find someone who can do it for $200, they're either cutting corners or losing money. Either way, you'll feel it in the service.
>
> I'm confident we're worth $400/location. But if that's not in the budget, I respect that. Maybe we're not the right fit right now."

**Robert:** "...Let me think about the $400 option."

**You:** "Of course. Just so you know, that annual prepay offer expires Friday. After that, it's month-to-month at $547. Want me to send you the details?"

### Success Criteria
- [ ] Didn't immediately cave
- [ ] Stood firm on value
- [ ] Offered options, not discounts
- [ ] Was willing to walk away
- [ ] Created urgency

## PRACTICE DRILL FORMAT

### Solo Practice (5 minutes each)
1. Read the scenario
2. Record yourself responding
3. Listen back
4. Grade yourself on success criteria
5. Do it again, improving weak points

### Partner Practice (10 minutes each)
1. Partner reads scenario setup
2. You respond live
3. Partner gives feedback
4. Switch roles
5. Debrief together

### Shadow Practice (15 minutes)
1. Listen to recording of great sales call
2. Pause after each section
3. Predict what they'll say next
4. Compare to actual
5. Note differences

## SCORING SYSTEM

Grade each role-play on:

| Criteria | 1 (Poor) | 2 (Fair) | 3 (Good) | 4 (Excellent) |
|----------|----------|----------|----------|---------------|
| Discovery | No questions asked | 1-2 questions | 3-4 questions | All 5 questions |
| Listening | Talked 70%+ | Talked 50-70% | Talked 40-50% | Talked <40% |
| Objection Handling | Got defensive | Gave up | Addressed well | Turned into opportunity |
| Closing | No ask | Weak ask | Good ask | Assumed close |
| Confidence | Sounded unsure | Some hesitation | Confident | Extremely confident |

**Target score: 14+ out of 20**
